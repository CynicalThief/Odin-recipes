Grilled Volcano Pork!

"Pork so hot you might wanna stick your-" - [REDACTED]

This is one of the most delicious pork dishes I've ever eaten-just an absolute eruption of flavor! But it's not why we're calling this volcano pork. That's actually because the marinade I'm using here is loosely based on one from a restaurant named after a famous Indonesian volcano. Serve this over steamed or coconut rice.

Ingredients:

    • Meat:

    • 1 (2 1/2 pound) boneless pork loin roast, trimmed

    • Marinade:

    • Â¼ cup thinly sliced lemongrass
    • 1 (2 inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and thinly sliced
    • 3 Peppers, fresno, raw
    • 1 Pepper, serrano, raw
    • 1 large shallot, sliced (or red onions!)
    • 3 cloves garlic, peeled
    • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
    • 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
    • teaspoon ground cumin
    • 1 pinch cayenne pepper
    • 3 tablespoons brown sugar
    • 2 medium limes, juiced
    • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
    • 3 tablespoons fish sauce
    • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Directions:

  1. Step 1: Slice the pork loin across into 1/2-inch thick pieces. Place into a large plastic zip-top bag.
  2. Step 2: Add lemongrass, ginger, Fresno chiles, serrano chile, shallot, garlic, turmeric, pepper, cumin, cayenne, brown sugar, lime juice, soy sauce, fish sauce, and oil to a blender or food processor, and blend on high until smooth.
  3. Step 3: Pour marinade into the bag of pork and massage thoroughly and toss until it's evenly coated. Seal the bag, squeezing out excess air as you do.
  4. Step 4: Transfer to the refrigerate and marinate overnight, or up to 24 hours.
  5. Step 5: Preheat an outdoor grill to high heat. Remove pork from bag, wiping off most of the excess marinade.
  6. Step 6: Cook on the preheated grill until an instant-read thermometer inserted into the center reads at least 145 degrees F (63 degrees C), 3 to 4 minutes per side.
